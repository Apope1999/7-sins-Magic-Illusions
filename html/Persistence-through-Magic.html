Persistence through magic

Eventually a water dripping on a rock will wither away the rock. that water is inside of you, and the rock is blocking you from achieving your goals. You have to wither that rock away. The voice inside that constantly whispers "I can't" needs to be silenced. Persistence is not something you do with external goals, but it is something you do internally. How learning magic will increase your persistence

The process of learning the art of impossibility will require the fundamental aspects that persistence runs through. Exercising magic will require time management, practise, idea execution, strength and calmness. Starting with time management and practise it is essential for you to understand that magic needs years to master in order to be used proffesionaly. But the goal is not always moneymaking so if you enjoy the art and you think it enlightens you, take your time. Through the process you will find yourself more aware of how the build up leads to terrific outcome, in other words how useful persistence over a task can be.

Moving on to idea execution, on its own it wouldn't stand for an aspect of persistance but it certainly is an enemy of procrastination. Persistence is built upon continouus activity. For example if you stumble upon a magic trick you enjoy, get into practising immediately (except if its dangerous). This way you will not allow yourself second thoughts about its impossibility. Remember you are learning the art of impossibility. Lastly, magicians are entertainers. Through practising magic tricks you will understand how physics work, how humans react to physics and psychology. These qualities will elevate you internaly and fill your everyday life with knowledge about the "workings" of the world. Strength and calmness are two key points in performing in front of an audience if you consider following the way of the magician.
