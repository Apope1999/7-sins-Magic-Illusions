Magic tricks and illusions in Antiquity

Magic is undoubtedly an ancient art. The first "magicians" to exist were the priests of the various religions that had developed throughout the ages in human society. They most often attributed their magic to some higher power granting them unnatural abilities, much different to the concept of modern magic that exists today.
The earliest reported magic trick (the ‚Äòcup and balls‚Äô trick) is almost 5,000 years old (2,700 BC) by Dedi in ancient Egypt. The same trick was performed over 2,000 years ago in ancient Rome where it was also known as acetabula et calculi by the Accetabularii (hence their name).Acetabularii are a group of magicians specializing on the cups and balls effect.

The cup and ball trick has been used for centuries since by street hustlers to con people out of money. A lot of ancient magic came out of tricks used to cheat people at gambling. Playing cards have long been used in magic tricks. Not much is known about the history of magic during the dark ages (400-1000 A.D.) , but much of it was associated with the occult and magic as entertainment is not prominent.
