Lance Burton

William Lance Burton was born on March 1960 in Columbia Kentucky. He became interested in magic at the age of 5 when he was one of the volunteers at a magic show of Magician Harry Collins during a Christmas party at the Frito-Lay plant where his mother worked. The young Burton was fascinated, and a neighbor, hearing of his interest, gave him a book, Magic Made Easy, which her own (then-grown) children had used. It contained ten tricks, all of which young Burton quickly learned. His first performances were for neighborhood children, charging them a nickel each.

Collins, a full-time magician, noticed the interest, and became his mentor when Burton was in his teens, teaching him the fundamentals of the trade. In 1977, as a teenager, Burton entered his first magic competition and won first prize. In 1980, shortly after his 20th birthday, he was awarded a "Gold Medal of Excellence" from the International Brotherhood of Magicians. Soon after, Burton moved to Southern California, where within a week he appeared on The Tonight Show.

In 1982, he competed at the international Fédération Internationale des Sociétés Magiques (FISM) competition in Lausanne, Switzerland, and won the "Grand Prix" prize, (the main event of the FISM competition). He has been awarded with the "David Devant" Award from the Magic Circle, which is given to those who have made a significant contribution in advancing the art of magic. Also he has been awarded twice the "Magician of The Year" award by the Academy of Magic Arts, and also a Masters Fellowship.

In 2010, Lance Burton ended his 31 year career and retired after he had performed more than 15,000 shows in Las Vegas for over 5,000,000 people.
