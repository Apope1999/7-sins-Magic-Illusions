David Copperfield

David Copperfield, borned with the name of David Seth Kotkin, on September 16, 1956, in Metuchen, Jersey. David Copperfield is one of the most famous magicians and wealthiest entertainers. Copperfield got started with magic at the age of 12, when he became the youngest person to gain admission into the Society of American Magicians.

By the age of 16, he was teaching a course in magic, at New York University and performing under the name David Copperfield, after the title of the eighth novel of Charles Dickens. After a brief pause at Fordham University, Copperfield was chosen as the lead in the Chicago musical "The Magic Man".

Copperfield had a successfull career with the ABC show "The Magic Man", in which he performed his magic tricks more than 500 times a year. Copperfield's most popular tricks involve walking through the Great Wall of China, making the Statue of Liberty Dissappear and escaping over a flaming raft over Niagara falls.

Throughout his long career, Copperfield has earned 21 Emmy Awards, been named "Magician of the Century" and "Magician of the Millenium", reveived the first star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame awarded to a living magician and received the prestigious U.S. Library of Congress' Living Legend Award. Moreover, Copperfield was knighted by the French government, becoming the first magician to receive the "Chevalier of Arts and Letters".

According to Forbes magazine, Copperfield earned $57 million in merchandise and tour revenue in 2005 alone. By 2012, the magician's net worth was estimated at $150 million, and his ticket sales had grossed an estimated $3 billion.
